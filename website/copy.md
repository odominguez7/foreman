## What it does

Today, every RFQ eats 30 to 90 minutes of your morning. You open the PDF. You look up the material. You check the schedule. You pull what you charged this customer last time. You write the quote in a spreadsheet or an email. You have been doing this for forty years and it still takes forever.

Foreman does that same work in under three minutes. You drop the drawing in. You tell it the customer and the quantity. It gives you back a draft quote with the math shown. You review it, change what needs changing, send it. Foreman never pretends to be you. It just has the quote ready by the time you sit down.

## How Foreman works

**1. Drop the drawing in.** Foreman reads the PDF, even Spanish or old scanned drawings. It pulls out the material, the tolerances, the features.

**2. Checks your shop.** Do you have 6061 in stock? Is the mill open next week? What did you charge Boeing last August for a bracket like this?

**3. Writes the quote.** The price comes from your own job history, not a calculator on the internet. The reasoning is shown next to the number. You can argue with it if you want.

**4. Remembers what you say.** Tell Foreman "Boeing pays slow, add 8 percent." Next week, when another Boeing job comes in, the 8 percent is already there. Nothing to update, no spreadsheet to find.

This last part is the one that matters. Every other quoting tool asks you to configure a policy once and then ignores you. Foreman learns the way a new foreman would: by listening to you every day.

## Three modes

Think of Foreman as a dial. Same drawing, three different quotes depending on how you feel about the job that day.

- **Careful.** Adds a margin cushion. Flags tight tolerances. Asks a question before it commits. Use this on jobs you really want to get right.
- **Normal.** Standard pricing off your history. Use this most days.
- **Aggressive.** Cuts the margin to win volume. Points at the job you lost last quarter so you remember what the market paid. Use this when the shop is slow.

Flip the dial. The next quote changes.

## Common questions

**Is this just ChatGPT in a box?**
No. ChatGPT does not know your inventory, your schedule, or what you charged Boeing last August. Foreman does, because it reads your shop's own records.

**Will Foreman replace my estimator?**
No. Foreman drafts; your estimator reviews and sends. Same person, more quotes out the door.

**What if the drawing is in Spanish or German?**
Foreman handles mixed-language drawings. Tested on real ones from real customers.

**What if the internet goes down?**
Foreman keeps working. The brain runs on the computer in your shop.

**What if I don't like a quote Foreman wrote?**
Say so in plain English. Foreman remembers your correction and applies it next time.

**What if the job is unusual and there is no history for it?**
Foreman tells you that. It falls back to shop-average pricing and flags what it is unsure about. It will not make numbers up.

**Is Foreman something I have to learn?**
No. You type to it the way you would text a person. No menus, no dashboards, no new software to learn. If you can text, you can run Foreman.
