I spent 11 years running the CNC shop my father built.

The job I hated most was quoting. Every RFQ that came in took 30 to 90 minutes of my morning. Open the PDF. Look up the material. Walk the floor and check the schedule. Dig through old quotes for what we charged this customer last August. Type it into a spreadsheet. Send. Hope it's right.

We lost good work because we couldn't answer fast enough. The shops that quoted in two hours got the job. The ones that quoted in two days didn't.

I came to MIT AI Studio (MAS.664) looking for a way to fix this. Today I'm shipping what I built.

It is called Foreman.

Foreman reads a customer drawing (PDF, scan, even Spanish or German). It checks your inventory and your schedule. It pulls your three closest historical jobs for that customer and material. It writes a draft quote in under three minutes, with the math shown. You review, edit, send.

The part I am most proud of: it remembers. Tell Foreman "this customer pays slow, add 8 percent." Next job from them, the 8 percent is already there. The way a real foreman would learn from you, every day.

Foreman runs on a small computer in your shop. No cloud, no monthly fee, no internet required. Your drawings stay in your building. Open source under MIT license.

If you run a shop and want to see Foreman quote one of your own drawings live, write to me. 20-minute call. No slides. Just your drawing and a quote.
